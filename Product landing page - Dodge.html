Dodge is an American brand of automobiles and a division of Stellantis, based in Auburn Hills, Michigan. Dodge vehicles have historically included performance cars, and for much of its existence Dodge was Chrysler's mid-priced brand above Plymouth. Founded as the Dodge Brothers Company machine shop by brothers Horace Elgin Dodge and John Francis Dodge in the early 1900s,[2] Dodge was originally a supplier of parts and assemblies to Detroit-based automakers like Ford. They began building complete automobiles under the "Dodge Brothers" brand in 1914, predating the founding of Chrysler Corporation. The factory located in Hamtramck, Michigan was the Dodge main factory from 1910 until it closed in January 1980. John Dodge died from the Spanish flu in January 1920, having lungs weakened by tuberculosis 20 years earlier.[3] Horace died in December of the same year, perhaps weakened by the Spanish flu, though the cause of death was cirrhosis of the liver. Their company was sold by their families to Dillon, Read & Co. in 1925 before being sold to Chrysler in 1928. Dodge mainstay vehicles were trucks and full-sized passenger cars through the 1970s, though it also built compact cars such as the 1963-76 Dart and midsize cars such as the "B-Body" Coronet and Charger from 1965-78. The 1973 oil embargo caused American "gas guzzler" sales to slump, prompting Chrysler to develop the Dodge Aries K platform compact and midsize cars for the 1981 model year. The K platform and its derivatives are credited with reviving Chrysler's business in the 1980s. One example was the Dodge Caravan. During the 1990s the Dodge Stratus found many buyers along with the larger Dodge Intrepid. The Dodge brand endured multiple ownership changes at Chrysler from 1998 to 2009, including its merger with Daimler-Benz AG from 1998 to 2007,[4] its subsequent sale to Cerberus Capital Management, its 2009 bailout by the United States government, and its subsequent Chapter 11 bankruptcy and acquisition by Fiat. In 2011, Dodge and its sub-brands, Dodge Ram and Dodge Viper, were separated. Dodge announced that the Viper was to be an SRT product, and Ram a standalone marque. In 2014, SRT was merged back into Dodge. Later that year, the Chrysler Group was renamed FCA US LLC, coinciding with the merger of Fiat S.p.A. and the Chrysler Group into the corporate structure of Fiat Chrysler Automobiles. Subsequently, another merger occurred on January 16, 2021 between FCA and the PSA Group (Stellantis), making the Dutch-domiciled automaker the second largest in Europe, after Volkswagen.

Dodge challenger:

The Dodge Challenger R/T features a 5.7-liter Hemi V8 engine, a performance exhaust, and an upgraded rear axle.
The Hemi is rated at 375 horsepower with a standard six-speed manual transmission,
and it's 372 horsepower with an optional eight-speed automatic.

Dodge challenger features:

  • Engine Lineup:

    • When you talk muscle cars, you first have to talk about the muscle. The Challenger has five engines to choose from, starting with the base 3.6-liter Pentastar V-6 engine. Dodge pairs this with its TorqueFlite eight-speed automatic transmission to get 303 horsepower and 268 pound-feet of torque. The R/T trim uses a 5.7-liter HEMI V-8 engine that ups the power game to 375 horsepower and 410 pound-feet of torque. The R/T Scat Pack trim uses Dodge’s 392 HEMI V-8 engine. This powertrain gets a best-in-class 485 horsepower and 475 pound-feet of torque. Not to be outdone, the SRT Hellcat comes with a 6.2-liter supercharged HEMI V-8 engine that delivers an impressive 717 horsepower and 656 pound-feet of torque. A high-output version of this 6.2-liter engine in the SRT Hellcat Redeye trims cranks out a monstrous 797 horsepower and 707 pound-feet of asphalt-tearing torque.

  • Widebody Package:

    • A Widebody Package that combines performance with attitude is standard on the SRT Super Stock and available on the R/T Scat Pack, SRT Hellcat, and SRT Hellcat Redeye trims. The Widebody Package includes 20-inch wheels wrapped in Pirelli P Zero tires for maximum lateral and straight-line grip. Its broader stance features flared fenders for a stylish appearance. Brembo performance brakes and an adaptive damping suspension system keep all that power under control.

  • Power Chiller:

    • The SRT trims feature a power-chiller that diverts air conditioning refrigerant from the cabin through a low-temp radiator at the front of the vehicle and then to the chiller unit. Once cooled, the air travels to the heat exchangers, which significantly cools intake air for increased engine performance. A line-lock feature locks the front tires but keeps the rear tires free to burn some rubber. Your smoke shows will be the envy of everyone.

  • Launch Control and Launch Assist:

    • A launch control system lets you set your engine to its optimal rpm and waits for you to release the brakes. The Challenger’s torque management system optimizes maximum straight-line traction and limits wheel slip. The launch assist system monitors wheel slips to resolve wheel hop issues. When detected, the system reduces torque without you having to raise the throttle, lessening driveline loads by up to 20%.

  • Race Cool Down:

    • After shutting the engine down, your race cool-down feature keeps the cooling fan and low-temp coolant pump running. This feature allows the supercharger to lower its air cooler temperature. You can track the temperature on the SRT Performance Pages to determine when your supercharger has reached its optimal temperature for another race. Dodge charger features:

  • Automatic High-Beams:

    • The daytime running lamps and automatic high beams/low beams on the 2021 Dodge Durango are incredibly bright. This helps you stay visible on the road to other drivers and allows you to see with ease, even in dark or inclement weather conditions. You don’t have to worry about high-beaming other cars, either. The inside rearview mirror has a camera that automatically detects the headlights of another vehicle and will switch to low beams right away until it passes by.

  • Trailer Sway Control:

    • The 2021 Durango is also the perfect choice for towing virtually anything you need. If you decide to opt for the Dodge Durango SRT Hellcat, you can tow up to 8,700 lbs., and it comes equipped with trailer sway control. This feature allows the trailer to stay sway-free because it detects excessive sway on the road and will correct it. It achieves correction by applying brake pressure to alternative wheels while simultaneously adjusting the engine’s throttle so that you can stay safely and securely in control the entire time.

  • Adaptive Cruise Control:

    • The 2021 Durango has the option for Adaptive Cruise Control with Stop, which automatically sets a safe speed when the lane ahead of you on the road is clear. However, if traffic slows down or stops, the throttle automatically adjusts, and the brakes are applied. This ensures there’s a safe distance maintained both behind your Durango and the vehicle in front of you at all times.

  • Rain-Sensing Wipers:

    • The latest Durango also comes with Rain-Sensing Windshield Wipers, which activate automatically at the first touch of rain. This is perfect for road splash and rainy conditions, so the windshield is crystal clear at all times, providing excellent visibility.

  • Optimal Weight Distribution:

    • The newest Dodge Durango has an innovative design with an optimal weight distribution of 52/48 between the rear-drive axle and engine. This is a near-perfect distribution that provides the Durango with increased downforce. Those properties allow for better traction, too.

  • Forward Collision Warning and Active Braking:

    • The new Durango also has an available option for the Forward Collision Warning with Active Braking. This advanced system gives audio and visual warnings if you’re too close to the car in front of you. Then, it will automatically apply the brakes to help prevent a collision, keeping you and your passengers safe.
